What is community smells?

Community smells are powerful indicators of costly sub-optimal conditions within your project's development communities. They act as a radar, highlighting non-cohesive team structures and communication or coordination issues that might lead to social debt.

About

Community smells are telltale signs of sub-optimal organizational and social issues lurking within software development communities. These issues often lead to increased project costs and a decline in software quality. Through extensive research, we've identified a range of community smells linked to less-than-ideal patterns in organizational-social structures within software development communities. 

To help you detect these community smells and gain valuable insights into your project's organizational-social structures, we proudly present csDetector - an open-source tool designed to automatically spot community smells and provide relevant socio-technical metrics. Leveraging advanced machine learning techniques, csDetector learns from existing bad community development practices, enabling it to deliver automated support in spotting related community smells. 

To prove the effectiveness of csDetector, we conducted evaluations on a benchmark of 143 open-source projects from GitHub. The results were impressive, with csDetector successfully detecting ten commonly occurring community smells in open software projects, achieving an outstanding average F1 score of 84%.
